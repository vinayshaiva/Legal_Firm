Justice is a multifaceted and fundamental concept that encompasses fairness, equity, and the application of moral and legal principles to ensure that individuals receive what they are due, both in terms of their rights and responsibilities. It is a cornerstone of any just and orderly society. Here's a more detailed description of justice.

Truth is the objective state or quality of being in accordance with fact or reality. It is the absence of falsehood or deception, representing an accurate and honest portrayal of information or events. Truth serves as a foundation for trust, transparency, and ethical conduct in various aspects of life.

Justice embodies the principles of fairness, equality, and the impartial application of laws and moral standards. It ensures that individuals receive their due rights and that wrongs are addressed appropriately. Justice strives to create a balanced and harmonious society where individuals are treated equitably and where the rule of law prevails.

Law is a system of rules and regulations established by a governing authority to maintain order, resolve disputes, protect individual rights, and promote justice within a society. It serves as a framework for behavior and interaction among individuals, organizations, and governments.

"Justice consists not in being neutral between right and wrong, but in finding out the right and upholding it, wherever found, against the wrong."
